PM Modi to hold public meeting for women in Kashi on Sat
[Image]
VARANASI:
Prime Minister Narendra Modi is set to visit his parliamentary constituency in UP's Varanasi. He introduced the historic "Nari Shakti Vandan Adhiniyam (women reservation bill)" and will address a public meeting exclusively for women at Sampurnanand Sanskrit University ground on Saturday.
[Image]
[Image]
This event was added to Modi's proposed Varanasi visit on Thursday afternoon. It triggered swift action from not only the district administration and police officials but also the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) cadres.
[Image]
[Image]
The Lok Sabha had recently passed the women's reservation bill, also known as Nari Shakti Vandan Adhiniyam, with an overwhelming 454-2 majority. This bill proposes to provide 33% reservation to women in the lower house and legislative assemblies.
[Image]
Divisional commissioner Kaushal Raj Sharma stated, "As per the initial itinerary, a public meeting of the PM was proposed after laying the foundation of the international cricket stadium at Ganjari and other events at Rudraksh International Convention Centre. On Thursday, one more event was added to PM's almost seven-hour visit. Now, he will also address a meeting in which only women will participate at SSU ground."
[Image]
[Image]
After the foundation-laying ceremony of the stadium and addressing a public meeting, Modi will proceed to SSU ground. Officials have indicated that the SSU meeting will be exclusively for women, with only the PM and Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath present on the dais.
[Image]
[Image]
Following this event, the PM will head to Rudraksh to distribute prizes among the winners of Kashi Sansad Sanskritik Mahotsava and interact with the students of Atal Residential Schools of Uttar Pradesh after dedicating the schools to the nation.
