There is degree of prejudice in Trudeau's allegations: MEA
NEW DELHI: India on Thursday said there is a degree of prejudice in Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau's allegations over the killing of Khalistani terrorist Hardeep Singh Nijjar and termed his charges as "politically motivated".The ministry of external affairs said Canada did not share any specific information with India on the Hardeep Singh Nijjar case.Najjar, who was a designated terrorist in India, was gunned down outside a Gurdwara, in a parking area in Canada's Surrey, British Columbia on June 18."We are willing to look at any specific information that is provided to us, but so far we have received no specific information from Canada," ministry of external affairs spokesperson Arindam Bagchi said on Thursday answering queries.Expect reduction in Canada's diplomatic presence in India The MEA has hinted at reducing the number of Canadian diplomats in India. "Canadian diplomatic presence in India is higher than that of India in Canada. We feel there should be parity of strength, rank equivalence by both sides," MEA spokesperson said. 'We expect Canada to ... 'The MEA said that New Delhi expected Canada to address India's concerns over terrorism and anti-India activities from the Canadian soil. We provided specific information with Canada on anti-India activities carried out from Canadian soil, the MEA spokesperson said. Temporary suspension of visas to cover Canadian applicants in third countries tooVisa services to Canadians have been suspended till further notice. The MEA said that the Indian high commission, consulates in Canada were temporarily unable to process visa applications as work has been disrupted due to security issues. The temporary suspension of visas to cover Canadian applicants in third countries too, the MEA said.
